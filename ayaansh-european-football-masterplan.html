🇪🇸 How Spanish Football Works — The Complete Pathway

Spain’s youth system is built around “canteras” (quarries) — each club mines young talent through a structured age-group pyramid. Here is how it works from age 13 to professional contract.

Introduction to team play. Fun-based learning.

⚪

Technical foundation. Positional awareness begins.

💡 Key Insight: The “Convenio” System

In Spain, players under 16 cannot sign professional contracts. Instead, they sign a “convenio de formación” (training agreement) which binds them to the club but doesn’t pay a salary. At 16, the club offers a professional contract or releases the player. If released, the player is FREE to sign anywhere — there’s no transfer fee owed. This means: if Ayaansh joins at 13-14, he trains for free. At 16, if the club wants to keep him, they MUST offer a professional contract with a salary. If they don’t, he’s free to go elsewhere. The risk is low.

💡 Key Insight: The Italian “Vincolo Sportivo” (Abolished)

Italy used to have a system called “vincolo sportivo” which bound young players to clubs indefinitely. This was abolished. Now, Italian youth contracts follow EU law: under-16 players sign training agreements (like Spain’s convenio). At 16, clubs can offer a “contratto di formazione” (formation contract) for 3 years max. At 19, the player must receive a full professional contract or become a free agent. FIGC rules also require clubs to provide education support for all academy players under 18. This is legally enforced.

🕵 The Scout & Agency Ecosystem

Understanding who the legitimate players are, how the industry works, and how to protect your family from scams while accessing real opportunities.

How the Football Scouting Industry Works

The Three Channels

There are exactly three ways a young player gets into a European academy:

1. Direct ContactBest

You email the club directly. Free. Most clubs have academy email addresses. This is what we recommend for Ayaansh.

2. Licensed Agent/ScoutGood

FIFA-licensed agents who have relationships with clubs. They take a commission (typically 5-10% of future contract). Only engage AFTER a club shows interest.

3. Trial AgenciesCaution

Companies that arrange trials for a fee. Some are legitimate, many are not. Always verify independently. Never pay more than €2,000 for a trial package.

The Economics

Understanding money flows in youth football:

Club charges you for trialsNever legitimate
Agent charges upfront feeRed flag
Agency arranges trial (€500-2K)Can be legit
Agent takes % of future contractStandard practice
Club covers trial costsBest case

Rule of thumb: A real club NEVER charges a player for a trial. If you’re paying, you’re paying an intermediary for access, not the club itself.

For Under-16 Players

Special rules for minors:

FIFA agent representationNot allowed under 16
Parent as representativeYes, always
Agent commission on minorsCapped by FIFA
Best approach for AyaanshDirect + agency backup

At 13, Ayaansh doesn’t need an agent. You (Anmol) act as his representative. If a club is interested, they deal with you directly. Only consider an agent AFTER a club makes an offer, to help negotiate contract terms.

⚠ Red Flags to Watch For

❌ “Guaranteed placement” — No one can guarantee a spot at any club. If they claim this, walk away immediately.

❌ Upfront fees over €3,000 — Legitimate trial arrangements cost €500-2,000 max (covers logistics). Anything more is a scam.

❌ No verifiable past placements — Ask for names of players they’ve placed. Google those players. If you can’t verify, it’s fake.

❌ Pressure to decide quickly — “Only 2 spots left” is a classic pressure tactic. Real opportunities don’t expire overnight.

❌ No FIFA license number — Every legitimate agent has a FIFA license. Ask for it. Verify at FIFA.com.

❌ Contact only via WhatsApp/Instagram — Legitimate agencies have offices, websites, and official email addresses. Social media-only operations are suspect.

FIFA Regulations & Visa Pathways

Critical legal framework for international transfer of minors under 18.

FIFA Article 19 — The Rule

FIFA prohibits international transfer of players under 18 years old. This is an absolute rule with only 5 narrow exceptions. Without an approved exception, no club can register Ayaansh.

Exception #1: Parental Relocation (YOUR PATH)

If parents move to the club's country for non-football reasons (e.g., work, career), the child can register with a local club. This is the most commonly approved exception.

Requirements:

1. Both parents (or primary guardian) must relocate
2. Employment contracts proving career-driven move (NOT football-driven)
3. Housing proof in new country
4. School enrollment for the player
5. Health insurance documentation
6. Application to FIFA Players' Status Committee
7. International Transfer Certificate (ITC) from AIFF

Critical: Spain's May 2025 Visa Change

Spain removed eligibility for student visas for sports academies in May 2025. This means you cannot get Ayaansh a student visa to attend a football academy. The ONLY viable route is parent employment-based relocation, which then triggers the FIFA Article 19 exception naturally.

Scam Protection Guide

Critical awareness for Indian families seeking European football opportunities. Scammers heavily target Indian families.

Red Flags — AVOID

Upfront payment requests

Legitimate clubs NEVER charge for trials. FIFA rules state inviting clubs must cover travel, accommodation, and meals for trial players.

Contact via Instagram DM, WhatsApp, or personal Gmail

Real clubs only contact through official email (@villarrealcf.es, @sevillafc.es). Any agent using @gmail.com or social media DMs is suspect.

"Guaranteed professional contract" promises

No one can guarantee a contract before trials. This is the #1 manipulation tactic used against Indian families.

Pressure to decide quickly or pay via Western Union/crypto

Legitimate processes take weeks/months. Urgency is a manipulation tool.

Scouts "discovering" your child on social media

Real scouts attend recognized tournaments and work through official channels, not Instagram.

Green Flags — TRUST

Official club email domains

@valenciacf.es, @villarrealcf.es, @sevillafc.es, @acmilan.com, @inter.it, @atalanta.it

FIFA-licensed agents

Verify at: inside.fifa.com/transfer-system/agents — this is the ONLY official directory.

Club covers trial costs

Legitimate clubs that want to assess a player will cover travel and accommodation costs.

Established organizations with verifiable track records

FCV Football Academy, Spanish Soccer Trials, IDA Rome — all have verifiable histories and transparent processes.
